Police Nab the Make-Up Artist Who Made a Teen Look Old to Escape India

By Nemx Cami

July 27,2025,19:45PM

Share :

Hustle Always Leaves Fingerprints

“We wear masks to survive, some make them”
Make-up artistry isn’t just
about weddings and Instagram filters. In the underground hustle economy, it’s camouflage, identity forgery, and transformation rolled into one. The cops in India recently busted a make-up artist who aged up a teenager with prosthetics, wrinkles, and hair dye so he could slip through airport security and escape the country. The disguise worked—until it didn’t.

Hustle Time

The golden rule of side hustles:
do what you’re good at, then stretch it into what people are desperate enough to pay for. A make-up artist paints faces for models? Sure. But paint wrinkles onto a desperate runaway? That’s where skills meet black-market demand. Every legit trade has a dark-market twin. Graphic designer? Counterfeiter-in-training. Mechanic? Chop-shop king. Makeup artist? Professional shapeshifter. But here’s the catch: if your hustle depends on staying invisible, it’s already halfway dead. Cops have nothing but time, cameras, and suspicion. And once they catch you, the skill that made you cash will be the same skill that gets you cuffed.

Some Heat

Every side hustle sits on a spectrum
between “grandma approves” and “Interpol has your file.” The trick isn’t avoiding the hustle—it’s knowing where you stand on that spectrum and deciding if the payout justifies the fallout. People talk about passion projects and dream jobs, but the real hustlers know: "your skill is a tool, and the world is divided into those who sell their tool clean and those who sell it dirty." This make-up artist just proved the lesson: your gift can get you into Vogue—or into a holding cell. Pick wisely. Because in the end, there’s no such thing as a harmless hustle—only one that hasn’t burned you yet.
