It’s Not So Easy to Get a Job These Days, It’s Painful

By Nemx Cami

July 27,2025,19:45PM

Share :

No More Jobs

“A man with empty pockets will fill them one way or another—sometimes with food, sometimes with crime ”
The job market isn’t just “tight.”
It’s a stranglehold. Companies automate, outsource, or slice wages thinner than deli ham, and then they wonder why people are flipping tables instead of flipping burgers. You’ve got a whole generation sending out résumés into the void, waiting weeks for a rejection email that never comes. “We’ll keep your application on file,” translates to: you’ve been buried in the HR cemetery, kid.

Hustle Time

Let’s cut the corporate brochure crap :
People need to eat. Rent doesn’t stop because the “economy is restructuring.” Families don’t magically survive on motivational quotes. No jobs means people get creative: gig work, under-the-table shifts, flipping stolen iPhones, cooking meth in a bathtub, pawning grandma’s last piece of jewelry, or driving an unlicensed taxi for cash. These aren’t just crimes—they’re survival strategies dressed up in desperation. You don’t choose illegality, it chooses you when the fridge is empty. And all those talking heads on TV saying “just learn to code”—guess what? Not everyone wants to starve while waiting for a bootcamp certificate. Survival is the last human right. And when the “system” blocks survival, don’t be shocked when people jump the fence and grab what they need.

Some Heat

Society is engineered to make you desperate,
then punish you for the desperation. You can’t get a job without experience, you can’t get experience without a job, and even if you do, the paycheck doesn’t cover rent, let alone a pack of ramen. Meanwhile, billion-dollar corporations pay their CEOs in yachts and golden parachutes while telling the working stiff to “grind harder.” The system doesn’t collapse—it eats people alive. That’s why we’ve got entire underground economies running parallel to the “official” one. Black markets, hustles, scams—it’s not some moral failing, it’s arithmetic. When legal money is out of reach, illegal money becomes gravity. Jobs aren’t just scarce, they’re designed that way. Fewer jobs = cheaper labor = more desperation. It’s a rigged casino where the house always wins. And if you’re broke long enough, society stops seeing you as unlucky and starts seeing you as disposable. That’s the pain:not just being jobless, but being erased.
