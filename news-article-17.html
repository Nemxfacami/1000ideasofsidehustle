Black Friday turning into a Black Fraud Day, says UK cyber security chief

By Nemx Cami

July 27,2025,19:45PM

Share :

Black Fraud Day

“Every discount has a shadow price. While shoppers hunt for bargains, predators hunt for them. ”
Black Friday used to be
the annual stampede for cheap TVs and toaster ovens. Now? It’s open season for cyber crooks. The UK’s cyber security chief isn’t mincing words—this isn’t just a sale day anymore, it’s a scam buffet.

Hustle Time

Here’s how the predators play it:
fake sites cloned pixel-for-pixel from real retailers, phishing emails dressed up like receipts, bogus delivery texts, “exclusive deals” that never existed. The con artists have turned Black Friday into their own private payday. Every shopper frantically punching in card numbers to save a few quid is basically tossing chum into the water—and the sharks are circling. The scammers know the drill: speed, greed, distraction. People drop their guard , and boom—money siphoned off faster than you can say “checkout complete.”

Some Heat

The irony drips like oil:
consumers sprint into stores to avoid “missing out,” but the ones really cashing in are sitting behind screens, not cash registers. Call it what it is—Black Friday isn’t just commerce anymore; it’s camouflage. The predators don’t need to break into banks when they can break into your inbox. And while the headlines scream about “doorbuster deals,” the real headline is this—fraud is the doorbuster. The bad guys found a way to get paid, not by selling products, but by selling illusions. And in the chaos, they don’t just steal money—they steal trust. That’s the real cost of Black Friday.
