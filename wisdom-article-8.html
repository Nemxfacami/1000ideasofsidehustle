We all love food so much that guards let people steal so you can pay them

By Nemx Cami

July 27,2025,19:45PM

Share :

Food Money

“Food isn’t just fuel — it’s the most profitable addiction we politely call dinner ”
Food isn’t just survival,
it’s the one addiction nobody wants cured. You can quit smoking, drinking, even gambling — but you can’t quit eating. That’s why food is the most reliable hustle on earth. Whole empires are built on people needing a snack every damn day.

Hustle Time

Look at how it works:
supermarkets toss half their shelves into “loss prevention” budgets, because stealing is baked into the business model. They know guards won’t stop every thief — because you, the paying customer, are the real target. The money isn’t in catching crooks, it’s in pricing food so high that everyone else carries the load. That’s why popcorn is $8 at the movies, bottled water is marked up 500%, and delivery apps charge you more for a burger than the cow itself was worth. Food hustling goes beyond restaurants. There’s meal prep, vending machines, “healthy” snack subscriptions, organic scams with a pretty label. Even poverty is monetized — notice how corner stores in poor neighborhoods charge more for the same bag of chips? That’s not charity, that’s capitalism sharpening its teeth.

Some Heat

Here’s the big ugly truth:
food is the ultimate leash. Whoever controls it, controls you. That’s why farmers get squeezed, fast food chains get fat, and delivery apps own your midnight cravings. Hustlers in food don’t just sell calories — they sell convenience, comfort, identity. Vegan, keto, gluten-free, “clean eating” — all buzzwords to jack up the price of bread and lettuce. And you’ll pay. Because hunger isn’t optional. Food will always drain your pockets because it owns your biology. Hustlers know this, and that’s why they keep the guards watching the thieves — not to stop them, but to remind you: you’re next at the register.
